Overcoming the Bottleneck

A significant upgrade in the U220 is its host interfacing. To utilize 56 MHz of instantaneous bandwidth without dropping samples (overflows), massive data rates are required. Legacy systems using Gigabit Ethernet choke under this load. The U220's USB 3.0 and expansion interfaces provide the necessary pipeline.

  • ✓ USB 3.0 Type-C: Delivers 5 Gbps, essential for full-bandwidth streaming.
  • ✓ PCIe 2.0 x4 & SFP: Available via expansion boards for ultra-low latency.
  • ✓ Absolute Coherence: On-board GPS, 10MHz, and PPS inputs prevent phase drift across distributed systems.

Primary Deployments

Because of its balance of price, wideband performance, and MIMO architecture, the U220 is widely deployed across telecommunications, defense, and academia.

Cellular Testbeds

Prototyping 4G LTE and 5G NR base stations (eNodeB/gNodeB) and user equipment using stacks like srsRAN.

SIGINT & Counter-UAS

Monitoring ISM bands to detect, analyze, and mitigate drone telemetry and unauthorized video links.

Radar Research

Utilizing the 10MHz/PPS sync capabilities to build multi-node direction-finding (Angle of Arrival) systems.

Electronic Warfare

Generating reactive jamming waveforms or simulating GPS spoofing to test the resilience of autonomous systems.

Native Software Integration

The U220 excels by integrating into established, industry-standard ecosystems out of the box via the USRP Hardware Driver (UHD).
